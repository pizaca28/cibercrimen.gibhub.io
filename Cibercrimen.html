¬øWhat is Cyber Crime?

Also known as Cibercrimen in Spanish, means those criminal activities that happened in the digital ambit, using computers and world wide networks as principal tools. These ones elicit actions such as data breaches, identity theft, online fraud, hacking, malware distribution and cyber bullying. Cybercriminals have different motivations for example, ideological reasons, financial aid, etc. These crimes can range from relatively minor offenses like unauthorized downloading of copyrighted material to more serious and malicious acts. Here are four reasons cybercrime occurs.

1. Financial Gain: Many of them engage in illegal activities with the primary motive of financial gain.

2. Anonymity: Provided by the internet makes it easier for criminals to commit these crimes without being easily traced.

3. Vulnerabilities in Technology: New vulnerabilities emerge, cybercriminals often find weakness in software, networks, or hardware unauthorized access, steal data, etc.

4. Cybercriminals continuously develop and employ sophisticated techniques to evade detection and bypass security measures.

The impact of Cyber crime.

The impact of cybercrime extends far beyond financial losses, permeating various aspects of society and posing significant challenges to individuals, businesses, and governments worldwide. Cyberattacks can lead to breaches of sensitive data, financial fraud, identity and disruption of critical infrastructure. Cybercrime can affect customer trust and loyalty, leading to a decline customer base and revenue. For example, here are some ideas from how Cybercrime impacts customer relationships. In first place, customers may lose trust in a business that fails their personal information. in second place, cybercriminals gain unauthorized access to customer accounts and result negatively impacting the costumer experience and lead costumer dissatisfaction.

How to prevent Cyber Crime.

To prevent cybercrime, individuals and organizations must prioritize cybersecurity measures and adopt proactive strategies to mitigate risks. This includes implementing strong passwords, regularly updating software and antivirus programs, and exercising caution when clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown sources. Here are some tips many effective ways of preventing these:

A. Using strong passwords that are lengthy, complex, and not easy to crack.

B. Avoiding suspicious links and attachments in email messages.

C. Enabling multi-factor authentication (MFA) to add an extra layer of security.

This information is from CyberSecurity Exchange.

Types of Cyber Crime.

Cybercrime range across a spectrum of activities. At one end of the spectrum, cybercrimes involve fundamental breaches of personal or corporate privacy. These offenses include attacks on the integrity of information stored in digital repositories and the use of illegally obtained digital data to harass, harm, or blackmail individuals or organizations. The emergence of these new cyber capabilities has sparked intense debate.

For instance, consider the case of Pegasus spyware, developed by the Israeli cyber-intelligence firm NSO Group. NSO Group claims that Pegasus is exclusively sold to government security and law enforcement agencies for aiding rescue operations and combating various criminal activities such as money laundering, sex trafficking, drug trafficking, and terrorism. Here are the types of Cybercrime:

1. DoS Attacks: Are used to make an online service unavailable and take the network down by overwhelming the site with the traffic from a variety of sources.

From Microsoft Security page DDoS attacks: ‚ÄúAre wide-reaching, targeting all sorts of industries and company sizes worldwide. 2. Botnets: are networks from compromised computers that are controlled externally by remote hackers.

3. Identity Theft: Occurs when a criminal gains access to a user's personal information to steal funds, confidential information, or participate in tax or health insurance fraud.

4. Cyberstalking: involves online harassment where the user is subjected to a plethora of online messages and emails. Typically use social media.

5. Phishing: This type attack involves hackers sending malicious email attachments or URLs to users to gain access to their accounts or computers.

6. PUPs: PUPS or Potentially Unwanted Programs are less threatening than other cybercrimes, but are a type of malware.

7. Exploit kits need a vulnerability (bug in the code of a software) in order to gain control of a user‚Äôs computer.
